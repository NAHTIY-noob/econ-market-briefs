# Market Logic Notes

Mechanism-driven briefs on markets, economics, strategy, and structural change.

## About This Repository

This repository is a public portfolio of short-form analytical notes.  
It is designed to turn economic events, market movements, policy changes, and major structural trends into clear, mechanism-based analysis.

Rather than simply summarising news, each note aims to answer a deeper question:

**What is really going on underneath the surface, and why does it matter for markets, firms, policy, or long-run economic change?**

This repository reflects the type of thinking I want to develop over time:
- structured reasoning
- economic mechanism tracing
- pattern recognition across events
- market and policy interpretation
- commercially and intellectually useful writing

It serves as an evolving showcase of my work in:
- macro and market analysis
- sector and strategy thinking
- economic intuition applied to real-world events
- long-run structural themes such as AI, labour, energy, regulation, and incentives

## What Makes These Notes Different

Most public market notes stop at:
- what happened
- a brief explanation
- a quick opinion

This repository is built differently.

Each note tries to move through a fuller chain:

**event → mechanism → transmission → implication → what to watch**

That means I am not only interested in describing events, but in understanding:
- the incentives behind them
- the causal links that drive outcomes
- the hidden variables people often miss
- the implications for markets, business decisions, and policy

In that sense, this repository is not just a collection of notes.  
It is part of a longer-term intellectual project: building a way of thinking that connects economics, markets, strategy, and structural change.

## Core Note Structure

Most notes in this repository follow a structured template.

### 1. Event / Core Question
What happened, or what is the key question worth asking?

### 2. Core Intuition
A compressed version of the main idea.
If someone only reads three lines, they should still understand the central logic.

### 3. Key Mechanism
What is the most important economic or strategic mechanism behind the event?

### 4. Transmission Chain
A step-by-step chain showing how one shock, policy, narrative, or incentive travels through the system.

### 5. What Most People Miss
A section for second-order effects, hidden assumptions, tensions, or variables that are easy to overlook.

### 6. Market / Strategy / Policy Implication
Why this matters in practice:
- for asset prices
- for firms
- for policymakers
- for long-run structural positioning

### 7. What to Watch Next
The indicators, variables, or future developments that matter most.

### 8. My View
A concise personal judgment based on the above reasoning.

## Main Note Series

This repository is organised around several recurring series.

### 1. Market Logic Briefs
Short notes on major macro, market, and geopolitical developments.

These briefs focus on:
- inflation
- rates
- currencies
- commodities
- safe-haven narratives
- debt and fiscal credibility
- policy shocks
- market reactions to structural uncertainty

These are not meant to be pure trading notes.  
They are designed to show structured market understanding.

### 2. Mechanism Notes
Shorter analytical notes focused on one economic mechanism at a time.

Examples:
- why efficiency gains do not always reduce total resource use
- why safe-haven assets can still fall during panic
- why AI productivity gains do not automatically translate into wage gains
- how debt, confidence, and credibility interact

This series reflects my interest in turning abstract theory into applied reasoning.

### 3. Structural Change Notes
Notes focused on long-run transitions and major shifts in the economy.

Examples:
- AI adoption and labour-market restructuring
- energy transition and investment incentives
- demographic change and fiscal pressure
- regulation, industrial policy, and strategic competition

This series is especially important for my long-term project of understanding how large-scale structural changes reshape industries, firms, and careers.

### 4. Strategy and Sector Briefs
Notes on how broader economic and policy forces affect industries, business models, and strategic positioning.

Possible sectors include:
- energy
- finance
- AI tools and enterprise software
- education
- labour-intensive industries
- regulated sectors

The goal is to connect economics with business reality.
